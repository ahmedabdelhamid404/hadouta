# ADR-002: Egyptian Cultural Specificity is the Moat

**Status**: Accepted
**Date**: 2026-04-30
**Decision-makers**: Ahmed, Claude

## Context

Western personalized children's book platforms (Wonderbly, magicalchildrensbook.com, Magic Story) exist with strong tech and brands. Saudi competitor Qessati exists. Hadouta needs a defensible competitive position that resists their entry into the Egyptian market.

## Decision

The competitive moat is **Egyptian cultural specificity**, not technical novelty: themes, settings, character options, dialect, family structures, Islamic moral frameworks designed authentically for Egyptian families.

## Rationale

- Western players cannot replicate Egyptian cultural depth without years of context they don't have
- Qessati uses Saudi cultural framing; reads foreign in Egypt
- Egyptian gift-giving culture is uniquely receptive to family-focused, occasion-driven products
- Cultural moat compounds over time as we accumulate Egyptian-specific content; tech moats erode as APIs commoditize
- Egyptian Arabic dialect + MSA mix is a deliberate differentiator western products won't ever optimize for

## Consequences

- Marketing must lead with Egyptian-ness, not "AI personalized books"
- Content production requires Egyptian writers + illustrators (cost: ~10K EGP one-time partnerships)
- Product features (themes, settings, character options) must be Egyptian-first, not translated
- Internationalization (English, diaspora) deferred to v2+ after Egyptian moat is established
